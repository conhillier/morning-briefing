1. Russia kills 10 in second Kyiv strike this week

At least 10 people have been killed and 46 injured, including five children, in a second night of Russian strikes on Kyiv in a week, according to the capital's top military administrator Timur Tkachenko. Rescue efforts are continuing at over 20 locations, with residential high-rise buildings hit in two districts. Russian ballistic missiles struck several buildings, with Mayor Vitaly Klitschko reporting fires breaking out in some apartment complexes and warehouses and a garage workshop also damaged. The strikes come on the eve of the NATO summit in Turkey, where President Zelensky is expected to hold talks with President Trump.

Why it matters: Zelensky had warned hours before the strikes that intelligence indicated a second wave of Russian attacks was coming, and he has urged allies not to delay supplies of long-range missiles, writing: "Any delay with missiles for our air defense... means the loss of lives, and it encourages Russia to continue the war."

Source: BBC

2. Sky buys ITV broadcast arm for £1.6bn

Sky, owned by US telecoms giant Comcast, has announced a £1.6bn deal to buy ITV's broadcasting and streaming arm, creating the UK's biggest commercial broadcaster. Sky will pay £1.2bn in cash initially for ITV's media and entertainment business, including its free-to-air TV channels and the ITVX streaming platform, with a further up to £200m payable in the second half of 2028 depending on 2027 advertising revenues. As part of the deal, Comcast will sell its Love Productions business - which makes The Great British Bake Off - to ITV for £200m. The transaction does not include ITV Studios, the programme-making arm that has produced shows including Love Island and I'm a Celebrity.

Why it matters: Sky has committed to spending at least £2.1bn between 2028 and 2032 on the business as part of a long-term strategic partnership, signalling a major long-term bet on British commercial broadcasting.

Source: The Guardian

3. Super Typhoon Bavi batters US Pacific islands

Super Typhoon Bavi has made landfall on Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, packing winds of nearly 290km/h (180mph) and gusts of 350km/h, according to the US National Weather Service. The NWS warned the storm could cause "catastrophic" damage, with waves potentially nearly 11m (35ft) high, and an official told AFP they had received reports of "major damages" on the Northern Mariana Islands. On Saipan, wind gusts of more than 161km/h (100mph) were recorded at the airport, and many residents were already without power from the last super typhoon Sinlaku, which hit the islands in April, killing 17 people and causing about $1.5bn in damage. Guam, with a population of about 170,000, opened five evacuation centres with a maximum capacity of around 1,700.

Why it matters: Bavi will be the 11th category four or five tropical cyclone to hit US territory in the past decade - one more than the total recorded in the prior 57 years - with scientists saying climate change is making powerful typhoons more common.

Source: BBC

4. Venezuela quake death toll passes 3,000

Venezuela's information ministry reported on Sunday night that 3,342 people have been killed in the twin earthquakes that struck on 24 June, with the number of people injured passing 16,700. Forensic technician Joel Mirabal, who has worked for seven days straight, said: "Obviously, mass graves will have to be created. The collapse is massive, and the bodies are buried under many layers of debris."

Why it matters: Mirabal said he and other forensic technicians anticipate spending up to three months collecting bodies, and the number of bodies found is expected to soar as international rescue teams wrap up survivor operations.

Source: The Guardian

5. OPEC+ raises output for fifth straight month

Seven OPEC+ member countries - Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, and Oman - will raise oil output by 188,000 barrels per day from August, following a virtual meeting to review global market conditions. This is the fifth consecutive monthly production increase, continuing a gradual unwinding of cuts first announced in 2023. Brent crude futures for September delivery stood at $72 as of 02:01 GMT on Monday, below Brent's settlement price of $72.48 on February 27, the day before the US and Israel launched strikes on Iran.

Why it matters: Total OPEC+ production dropped to 33.13 million bpd in May, down from 42.77 million bpd in February, according to OPEC figures, as Iran's effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz forced members to slash output.

Source: Al Jazeera

Closer to Home

Robroyston grows fast but lacks doctors, schools

Glasgow's Robroyston suburb has seen explosive housing growth since the 1990s but residents say no amenities have followed, with locals reporting no doctors, no dentists and hardly any sports facilities despite hundreds more homes planned. Glasgow City Council identified Robroyston and nearby Millerston as a community growth area in 2009, with a masterplan listing about 1,600 homes, though the final number is now estimated at around 2,000. A key source of frustration is how section 75 money - around £15,000 per new home from developers - was largely absorbed by Robroyston train station, which opened in 2019 but which locals say has no transport links connecting homes to it. Glasgow list MSP Paul Sweeney said: "The train station has really soaked up the community growth fund money," though £5.4m of funding remains and will increase as more homes are sold.

Source: BBC
