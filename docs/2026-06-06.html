1. US shoots down Iranian missiles targeting Kuwait, Bahrain

The US military said it intercepted six of seven Iranian ballistic missiles fired toward Kuwait and Bahrain on Friday, with the seventh failing to reach its target. Earlier in the day, US forces had shot down four Iranian drones launched toward the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's Revolutionary Guard said it targeted the Ali Al Salem airbase hosting US forces in Kuwait and the US Navy's 5th Fleet in Bahrain. The US military struck Iranian coastal surveillance radar sites in response, saying it acted "to defend against further attacks."

Why it matters: The exchange of fire further frayed a shaky ceasefire with Tehran, with US and Iranian negotiators having reached only a tentative agreement a week ago to extend the ceasefire by 60 days.

Source: NPR

2. Xi Jinping to visit North Korea next week

Chinese President Xi Jinping will make a state visit to North Korea from Monday to Tuesday, his first trip to the country since June 2019, both countries announced Friday. The visit comes a day after North Korea unveiled a new facility believed to be a uranium enrichment plant, during which Kim Jong Un announced plans to bolster nuclear forces "at an exponential rate." Analysts say Kim is eager to cement his country's status as a nuclear weapons state ahead of Xi's visit. The trip follows Xi hosting both US President Trump and Russian President Putin in Beijing in quick succession.

Why it matters: As North Korea builds closer ties with Russia, China is seeking to use Xi's trip to reassert its influence over Pyongyang and safeguard its strategic interests in northeast Asia, according to analyst William Yang of the International Crisis Group.

Source: NPR

3. Google to pay SpaceX $920m a month for AI compute

Google has agreed to pay SpaceX $920 million a month for AI compute capacity at its data centers, spanning from October this year through June 2029. Google will use approximately 110,000 Nvidia GPUs as well as central processors, memory and other components housed in SpaceX's data centers. A Google Cloud spokesperson said the deal was made "to ensure we have bridge capacity to meet surging customer demand" for its Gemini Enterprise agent platform. The deal is SpaceX's second major infrastructure agreement after a similar arrangement announced with Anthropic last month.

Why it matters: The deal boosts SpaceX's AI revenue story ahead of a planned IPO next week at a valuation of over $1.75 trillion, while SpaceX's AI segment recorded an operating loss of $2.5 billion in the first quarter on just $818 million in revenue.

Source: CNBC

4. Raspberry Pi raises profit forecast on AI demand

Cambridge-based Raspberry Pi has raised its profit expectations, saying it expects adjusted earnings of at least $38m (PS28.2m) for the first half of 2026, driven by strong AI sector demand. The update sent shares up by as much as 25% in early Friday trading, lifting the company's market value to around PS2bn. The company said it expects more than four million units in sales for the half-year and is on track for earnings to be "significantly ahead of current market expectations" for 2026 as a whole. The firm has also seen the value of its stock more than triple since the start of the year.

Why it matters: The credit card-sized computers are increasingly being used to create AI-powered devices, offering a cheaper alternative to more specialised hardware, positioning Raspberry Pi as a beneficiary of rapid AI sector growth.

Source: BBC

Closer to Home

Coalsnaughton evacuees condemn substandard temporary housing

Residents of 97 properties evacuated from four streets in Coalsnaughton, Clackmannanshire, have issued a statement saying they have been "plunged into the unknown" with no confirmed cause of the ground movement and no clear return date. One resident, Emma Little, said she spent PS1,500 on decent accommodation after being offered student housing she described as "filthy" with stains on mattresses and mould around windows. First Minister John Swinney visited the affected streets on Friday and said the Scottish government had activated the Bellwin Scheme to provide emergency financial assistance to the council.

Source: BBC
