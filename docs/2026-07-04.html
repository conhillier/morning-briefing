1. Iran begins funeral of the century for Khamenei

Iran has begun several days of public mourning and funeral processions for its former Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, more than four months after he was killed in strikes launched by the US and Israel. Iranian authorities said 12 to 20 million people were expected to attend the ceremonies, which are being called the "funeral of the century". Khamenei's body is currently lying in state at Tehran's Grand Mosalla ahead of his burial in his hometown of Mashhad next Thursday. Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif, former Russian president Dmitry Medvedev and the Afghan Taliban's Foreign Minister Amir Khan Muttaqi were among those in attendance.

Why it matters: President Trump, speaking at Mount Rushmore on Friday night, acknowledged the week of mourning in Iran, saying the country was "dying to settle" as negotiations to end the war continue.

Source: BBC

2. Europe heatwave kills 2,000-plus in France alone

France recorded 2,025 excess deaths in the last week of June, nearly a 30% rise, with deaths in the Paris region alone up 62%, according to Public Health France. The country saw its hottest day ever on average country-wide on 24 June, with temperatures hitting almost 41C in Paris and half the nation placed under a red heat alert. Belgium recorded 1,222 excess deaths during the heatwave - 39% more than usual - while the Netherlands saw about 480 excess deaths. More extreme temperatures are now forecast for the UK, France and the Iberian Peninsula this weekend.

Why it matters: The French health ministry said the figure was likely an "underestimate" and mortality would "therefore be higher than these initial figures", while Meteo-France has issued red alerts for Friday and Saturday for forest fires in southern France.

Source: BBC

3. Monaco bomb suspect disguised as man, on the run

A Ukrainian woman, Anastasiia Berezovska, 39, is suspected of leaving a parcel bomb in the entrance hall of an apartment building in Monaco, where a sanctioned Ukrainian multi-millionaire, his partner and 13-year-old son were seriously injured. Monaco's deputy prosecutor said Berezovska was "disguised as a man" and is believed to have picked up a hire car after the attack and driven to Italy and on to Germany. An Interpol Red Notice has been issued for her, wanted for attempted murder, placing an explosive device on a public road with criminal intent and criminal conspiracy. German special forces searched the rented apartment of a 39-year-old Ukrainian woman in the Main-Taunus district, but she remains at large.

Why it matters: Prince Albert II of Monaco described the incident as a "heinous crime" and investigators are also looking for possible accomplices.

Source: BBC

4. Two Romanians jailed for Iran-ordered London stabbing

Nandito Badea, 21, and George Stana, 25, have been jailed at the Old Bailey for a "targeted" knife attack on Iranian TV journalist Pouria Zeraati outside his home in Wimbledon, which the judge said the "evidence overwhelmingly points" to having been carried out on behalf of the Iranian regime. Zeraati, who worked for Persian-language channel Iran International, was stabbed three times in the thigh on 29 March 2024. Stana was jailed for 12 years and Badea for 8 years after being convicted of wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm. Zeraati said in a victim impact statement the incident had left him "scared and anxious" and he had to relocate abroad "for fear of any reprisals".

Why it matters: Security minister Angela Eagle said the sentences "send a clear message: anyone acting on behalf of a foreign state to commit crimes in the UK will be identified, pursued and punished", and said the government is fast-tracking legislation to introduce new powers to clamp down on state-linked organisations and their proxies.

Source: The Guardian

5. Starling Bank cuts 130 jobs, bets on AI

Starling Bank has announced it will cut 130 jobs - 3% of its workforce - as part of a restructuring of its banking and tech operations, while increasing investment in artificial intelligence. The London-based fintech, which employs more than 4,000 people, said the restructuring was necessary to reduce "duplicate" roles and step up spending on AI. The cuts come after the bank reported a 6% drop in revenue to £887m in the year that ended in March, with pre-tax profit falling 3% to £217m.

Source: The Guardian

Closer to Home

Scottish firefighters join Venezuela quake rescue

Three Scottish firefighters - Gavin Brown, Ian Hodgson and Brian Richards - have been deployed to Venezuela as part of the UK's search and rescue operation following twin earthquakes that struck on 24 June, killing more than 2,000 people with tens of thousands still missing. The UK crew consists of 68 firefighters and staff from 14 fire and rescue services. Ian Hodgson, a crew commander based at North Anderson Drive station in Aberdeen, said the team has been using listening devices to detect "saveable lives" and identify how far away the person is. Brian Richards, a firefighter at MacAlpine Road station in Dundee, said resilience was a vital skill during long days and extreme temperatures.

Source: BBC

For CFOs - UK regulatory updates

Companies House accounts filing reforms delayed to April 2028

From April 2028, small companies and micro-entities must file profit and loss accounts with Companies House but may opt out of publication on the public register. All UK registered companies will be required to file accounts in iXBRL format via commercial software, with web and paper-based filing systems closing for accounts filings from that date. The package was deferred from April 2027 to April 2028, giving companies one full accounting year plus 9 months (21 months) to prepare.

Source: Companies House
