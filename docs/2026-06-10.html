1. Pakistan air raids in Afghanistan kill 13, mostly children

Afghanistan has accused Pakistan's military of killing at least 13 people, most of them children, in air attacks on the provinces of Kunar, Khost and Paktika. Taliban spokesman Zabihullah Mujahid said the victims included 11 children, one woman and an elderly man, with 14 other women and children wounded. An official in Khost province said a house in the Spera district was struck, killing nine people and wounding 10 others, while a separate attack killed three civilians in the Barmal district of Paktika. The attacks came a day after suspected TTP fighters attacked a security post in Pakistan's Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, killing six members of the Federal Constabulary.

Why it matters: The UN reported in May that cross-border fighting had killed at least 372 Afghan civilians and injured another 397 in the first three months of 2026, and a fragile ceasefire reached in March has already collapsed.

Source: Al Jazeera

2. SpaceX IPO locks price at $135 per share

SpaceX has set a fixed IPO price of $135 per share, implying an anticipated market cap of $1.77 trillion, ahead of its Nasdaq debut on Friday. Unlike a typical IPO, the company is offering a take-it-or-leave-it price rather than building demand through a price range, and plans to stop taking orders on Wednesday to allow all of Thursday for share allocation. SpaceX is targeting retail investors receiving roughly 30% of shares - amounting to about $22.5 billion - far above the typical IPO retail slice of 5% to 10%. The company generated $18.7 billion in revenue last year but recorded an operating loss of $4.2 billion.

Source: CNBC

3. Anthropic releases restricted public version of Claude Mythos

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 to the public on Tuesday, the first widely available version of its advanced Mythos AI class, which had been restricted to a small group of partner institutions since April over cybersecurity concerns. To date, groups using Mythos have reported finding more than 10,000 critical security flaws in their systems.

Why it matters: Anthropic is expected to become a public company soon as its private valuation has neared $1tn, and consistently showing broader AI capabilities underlines its value to investors.

Source: BBC

4. SoftBank plunges 10% as Asia tech stocks slide

Asian semiconductor and technology stocks resumed their slide on Wednesday, tracking overnight losses on Wall Street where the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.97% and the S&P 500 slipped 0.26%. Japan's SoftBank Group plunged 10% after efforts to secure at least $6 billion through a margin loan backed by its OpenAI stake hit a snag, with the firm exploring alternative funding options. In South Korea, memory chip major SK Hynix dropped over 8% while Samsung Electronics fell 7.45%, and in Taiwan, TSMC fell about 2%. AI-related fundraising appears to be diverting money away from existing technology stocks, with upcoming listings such as SpaceX, Anthropic and OpenAI potentially absorbing investor capital.

Why it matters: Equity strategist Andrew Jackson at Ortus Advisors said the latest volatility in technology shares could prompt investors to rotate into defense names, particularly in Japan, citing Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Kawasaki Heavy Industries, IHI Corp. and Japan Steel Works as potential beneficiaries.

Source: CNBC

5. China-linked hackers steal AI from US tech firms

Cybersecurity firm CrowdStrike warned on Tuesday that Chinese entities accounted for more than 58% of state-sponsored targeted cyberattacks aimed at tech companies, particularly targeting their AI assets. CrowdStrike also found North Korea-affiliated entities tried to infiltrate IT workforces across North America, Europe and Asia, primarily to generate revenue for the regime.

Source: CNBC

Closer to Home

Far-right protests flare across Glasgow after Belfast stabbing

Protests broke out across Scotland on Tuesday after disorder flared in Belfast following a knife attack in which the alleged attacker was described by police as a Sudanese man. Hundreds of demonstrators gathered at locations in Glasgow city centre with faces covered, chanting and carrying Union flags, while dozens marched along Princes Street in Edinburgh with blue flares, and about 100 staged a demonstration in Ayr. A large number of police officers were deployed in Glasgow, closing a number of streets and bridges and diverting protesters away from the city centre. The Glasgow branch of Stand up to Racism condemned "repugnant scenes" and described the gathering as "fascists and far right thugs," saying marchers were seen "stealing a bike from a delivery driver and beating up passers-by."

Source: BBC

For CFOs - UK regulatory updates

Companies House accounts filing reforms delayed to April 2028

From April 2028, small companies and micro-entities must file profit and loss accounts with Companies House but may opt out of publication on the public register. All UK registered companies will be required to file accounts in iXBRL format via commercial software, with web and paper-based filing systems closing for accounts filings from that date. The implementation date has shifted from April 2027 to April 2028, giving companies one full accounting year plus 9 months (21 months) to prepare.

Source: Companies House
