1. US-Iran talks end with '60-day roadmap'

The first round of negotiations between the US and Iran in Switzerland ended with mediators Qatar and Pakistan declaring "encouraging progress". In a joint statement early Monday, they said a "High Level Committee" had agreed to "a roadmap towards reaching a final deal within 60 days". The parties also agreed to form a "communication line" to avoid incidents in the Strait of Hormuz and to establish a "de-confliction cell" between the US, Iran and Lebanon to end military operations there. Iran's Foreign Minister Seyed Abbas Araghchi said the talks "delivered major progress" to end the conflict in Lebanon.

Source: BBC

2. Starmer set to announce exit timetable Monday

UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer is expected to set out a timetable for his departure on Monday, which would make him the country's seventh leader in a decade. A number of senior government ministers have reportedly urged Starmer to spell out an exit plan, including Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper, Home Secretary Shabana Mahmood, and Energy Minister Ed Miliband. A heavy defeat in last month's local elections, coupled with sustained poor opinion polling, has steadily eroded Starmer's authority over the ruling Labour Party.

Why it matters: Starmer's resignation would clear the way for Andy Burnham - due to be sworn in as a member of parliament later Monday - to succeed him as Labour leader and UK prime minister, with Burnham's sizable victory in last week's Makerfield by-election leaving him poised to enter Downing Street unopposed.

Source: CNBC

3. Qatar LNG blast injures 54, 18 missing

An explosion at Qatar's main liquefied natural gas processing facility at Ras Laffan Industrial City has injured 54 people and left 18 others missing, the Qatari Ministry of Interior said on Monday. The ministry blamed the incident on a "technical malfunction" and said the Qatari International Search and Rescue Group had been deployed for those missing. QatarEnergy said emergency response teams were immediately deployed after the explosion at the Barzan factory and brought a fire at the facility under control. The ministry said there was no leakage from the facility that would pose a danger to public safety.

Why it matters: Ras Laffan Industrial City, located about 80km north of Doha, is home to the world's largest LNG export facility, producing about one-fifth of global supply, meaning any disruption there has significant implications for global energy markets.

Source: Al Jazeera

4. Oil falls, stocks rise on Iran talk progress

Investors responded positively to reports of progress in the US-Iran peace talks, sending Brent crude prices down more than 2% to below $80 a barrel, at $78.90, a significant drop from its May peak of $126.41. Stock markets rose in Asia, with Japan's Nikkei up 1.8%, South Korea's Kospi up 0.6%, and Chinese blue chip stocks up 1.6%. European markets also opened higher, with Germany's Dax up 0.22% and France's CAC 40 up 0.15%. In a development critical to unlocking progress, the US Treasury was also preparing to issue a 60-day waiver lifting sanctions on oil, petrochemicals and derivatives, with Iran saying its central bank would be able to sell oil to customers and receive payments without threat of sanctions.

Source: The Guardian

5. Colombia election: right-wing de la Espriella leads

Right-wing populist candidate Abelardo de la Espriella has narrowly won Colombia's presidential election based on a preliminary "quick count", leading with 49.7% over left-wing opponent Ivan Cepeda's 48.7% with over 99% of the vote counted. De la Espriella, a 47-year-old millionaire businessman and criminal defense attorney who calls himself "El Tigre", was endorsed by President Donald Trump and has pledged a sweeping military offensive against guerrilla groups on his first day in office. The razor-thin margin means no final result has been certified, and his opponent's campaign is challenging results from around 33,000 ballot boxes. US Secretary of State Marco Rubio was among the first to congratulate de la Espriella, posting that "Colombia's best days are ahead."

Why it matters: The result reflects a broader rightward shift across Latin America, with far-right and center-right governments now holding power in countries including Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Costa Rica, Ecuador, El Salvador, Honduras, and Paraguay.

Source: NPR

Closer to Home

CCTV shows Edinburgh knife attack in five minutes

BBC Scotland has obtained CCTV footage showing a topless man driving erratically on Leith Walk before abandoning his car and attacking multiple people over the course of approximately five minutes on Friday night. The footage shows the man, with a cigarette in his mouth, striking at a black man with two large knives, then battering the door of a pizzeria, and subsequently attacking a delivery driver on an e-bike. Police said five people in total were hurt in attacks at various locations across Edinburgh, with four needing hospital treatment; two of the injured men, aged 22, had been attending prayer at Broomhouse Mosque about half an hour before they were attacked. Counter-terrorism police joined the probe after a 36-year-old man was detained and charged.

Source: BBC
