1. Iran Drones Kill One at Kuwait Airport

Iranian drones struck Kuwait International Airport on Wednesday, killing one person and injuring more than 60, with Kuwait's defence ministry calling it "criminal Iranian aggression". The person killed was later identified as an Indian citizen, and Kuwait's foreign ministry ordered two Iranian diplomats to leave within 24 hours. Iran's IRGC claimed the attack, saying it was in retaliation for US strikes on an Iranian oil tanker and Qeshm Island.

Source: BBC

2. Ukraine Drones Hit St Petersburg Before Forum

Ukraine carried out a drone strike on the outskirts of St Petersburg hours before the opening of Russia's flagship economic forum, with local authorities saying air defences shot down 59 drones overnight and that three districts were hit. Volodymyr Zelensky confirmed Ukrainian drones had struck several locations including an oil terminal and a naval base in Kronstadt, the main outpost of Russia's Baltic Fleet. The commander of Ukraine's unmanned systems said the corvette Boikiy was struck.

Why it matters: The strike hit Russia's second city as thousands of guests from 130 countries, including a low-key US delegation, were due to attend the St Petersburg Economic Forum.

Source: BBC

4. 21 Killed in Delhi Hotel Fire

At least 21 people were killed and dozens injured after a fire tore through a multi-storey building in the Malviya Nagar area of south Delhi. Many victims were foreign nationals, including people from South Asian and African countries who had travelled to India for medical treatment or to accompany relatives receiving care. The building allegedly operated as a bed-and-breakfast catering to patients near a private hospital. Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced compensation of 200,000 rupees for families of those killed and 50,000 rupees for those injured.

Why it matters: Delhi minister Ashish Sood said authorities were investigating whether the building had the necessary permissions to operate as a bed-and-breakfast, with those found responsible for violations facing criminal action.

Source: BBC

5. Nissan Eyes Chery Deal for Sunderland Plant

Nissan has signed a non-binding agreement to explore contract manufacturing for Chinese carmaker Chery at its Sunderland plant, the UK's largest car factory with around 6,000 workers. If the deal proceeds, Nissan would aim to begin manufacturing cars for Chery International UK at Sunderland's production line 1 in the 2027 financial year. Chery's Jaecoo 7 became the top-selling model in the UK in March. Nissan last month consolidated Sunderland production onto one of its two lines, opening the way for a Chinese entrant on the other.

Why it matters: The deal would safeguard jobs at Sunderland at a time when Nissan is going through a painful global restructuring process, including closure of several plants in Japan.

Source: The Guardian

Closer to Home

Man Admits Murdering Inverness Musician

Waldemar Jaronski, 52, of no fixed abode, pled guilty to murdering musician Iain MacFarlane, 32, at the High Court in Glasgow. Jaronski struck MacFarlane with a glass bottle before choking him with a strap and a vacuum cord at his flat in the Milton of Leys area of Inverness on 4 December last year. Judge Lord Mullholland described it as a "brutal murder of a defenceless man". Jaronski later called 999 and said: "I killed Iain. He is dead."

Source: BBC
