3. Zelensky allies set five conditions for peace

The leaders of Ukraine, the UK, France and Germany set out five conditions for a 'just and lasting' deal to end the war with Russia, following talks in London on Sunday. The conditions include a stop to the fighting, starting negotiations from the current position in the field, and 'robust' security guarantees for Ukraine. The leaders also reiterated the need for the US to be part of the process. Before arriving in London, Zelensky accused Russia of a 'vile' attack after a drone struck a storage facility for spent nuclear fuel near the Chornobyl nuclear plant, though there were no injuries and radiation levels remained stable.

Closer to Home

Tata Steel Port Talbot furnace faces eight-month delay

Plans for the £1.25bn electric arc furnace at Port Talbot may be delayed by up to eight months due to problems with electrical connectivity, Tata Steel has warned. Boss Koushik Chatterjee told investors that National Grid had formally alerted the company that its connectivity project is delayed, which he described as 'critical for Tata Steel UK for the project commissioning.' The furnace had been hoped to be up and running by the end of 2027, replacing the traditional blast furnaces which closed two years ago with the loss of 2,000 jobs. National Grid said the work involved constructing two new substations, installing transformers and laying 2km of underground cables, with delays due to ground conditions and environmental and planning considerations.

Source: BBC

For CFOs - UK regulatory updates

Mandatory tax adviser registration with HMRC begins 18 May 2026

From 18 May 2026, a new mandatory registration requirement for paid tax advisers interacting with HMRC on behalf of clients rolls out online in stages through to 31 March 2027. Different adviser groups have staggered three-month registration windows: new advisers from 18 May, those with Self Assessment or Corporation Tax accounts from 18 August, payroll-only advisers from 18 November, and existing agent services account holders from 31 December 2026. Eligible advisers who do not register by the relevant deadline will not be permitted to interact with HMRC on behalf of clients, and HMRC may apply sanctions including financial penalties if they continue to interact when told to stop.

Source: HMRC
