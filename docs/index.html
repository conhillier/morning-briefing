1. Israel and Iran Trade Missiles, Ceasefire Crumbles

Israel and Iran exchanged missile fire in the first such attacks between the two rivals since a Middle East ceasefire was declared two months ago. Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps said its missiles targeted three military air bases in Israel, two in the centre of the country and one in the north, while the Israeli military said it had shot down all incoming fire.

Why it matters: President Trump said "We are very close to a final deal with Iran. It is going to be a good deal. I don't want it to blow up because of what is happening now," signalling that the resumed strikes directly threaten ongoing diplomatic efforts to end the war.

Source: BBC

2. Asia Markets Dive on Tech Fears and Middle East

Asia's major stock markets dropped sharply on Monday as investors followed Wall Street's lead to unload high-priced tech equities linked to AI, compounded by the resumption of conflict between Israel and Iran. Chip giants Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix dropped 10.2 percent and 7.6 percent respectively.

Source: Al Jazeera

3. Philippines Earthquake Kills 19, Triggers Tsunami Alerts

A magnitude-7.8 earthquake struck off the coast of Mindanao island in the southern Philippines on Monday at 07:37 local time, killing at least 19 people and injuring at least 134 across several provinces. The quake triggered tsunami alerts in the Philippines, Indonesia, Japan and Australia, with a wave of up to 1.4m detected along some coastlines. More than 130 aftershocks, with magnitudes ranging from 1.3 to 6.7, were recorded after the initial quake, which also downed power and communication networks in the coastal province of Sarangani. Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr ordered the suspension of classes in affected areas, which coincided with the first day of the school year.

Source: BBC

4. Ukraine and Europe Set Five Conditions for Peace

Ukraine and its close European allies - the UK, France and Germany - set out five conditions for a "just and lasting" deal to end the war with Russia in a joint statement after talks in London. The conditions include a stop to the fighting, starting negotiations from the current position in the field, and "robust" security guarantees for Ukraine. The leaders also reiterated the need for the US to be part of the process at a time when President Trump's focus has shifted to the war with Iran. Before arriving in London, Zelensky accused Russia of a "vile" attack after a drone struck a storage facility for spent nuclear fuel near the Chornobyl nuclear plant, though there were no injuries and radiation levels remained stable.

Why it matters: The leaders said Ukraine's right "to choose its own security arrangements and alliances must be fully respected," in an apparent pushback against Moscow's insistence that Ukraine abandon its ambition to join NATO.

Source: BBC

5. Xi Jinping Arrives in North Korea on Rare Visit

Chinese President Xi Jinping arrived in Pyongyang on Monday for a two-day state visit, his first trip to North Korea in seven years. Xi and his wife, Peng Liyuan, were welcomed at the international airport by North Korean President Kim Jong Un and his wife, Ri Sol Ju, amid a 21-gun salute and the fanfare of a military band. Experts say China is likely seeking to reassert its influence over North Korea to prevent it from leaning too heavily towards Moscow, with potential offers including shipments of rice and fertilisers and a resumption of Chinese group tourism to North Korea.

Why it matters: Pyongyang is heavily dependent on its northwestern neighbour for as much as 95 percent of its trade, but that relationship has shifted since Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022, as North Korea provided Moscow with critical weapons, artillery and manpower.

Source: Al Jazeera

Closer to Home

Starmer Gives Apple and Google September Deadline on Child Explicit Images

Prime Minister Keir Starmer told tech companies on Monday they have until September to install software that blocks explicit images on children's mobile phones or face legislation enforcing the requirement. Speaking at London Tech Week, Starmer said companies must activate nudity-detection algorithms or other technical solutions to prevent users taking photos or sharing images of genitalia unless they are verified as adults. If businesses do not comply within three months, legislation will be brought forward requiring the protection to be added to all phones and tablets sold in the UK. Starmer said the plan would mean the UK becomes "the first country in the world to make it impossible for children to take, share or view nude images."

Source: The Guardian

For CFOs - UK regulatory updates

Mandatory tax adviser registration opens 18 May 2026

From 18 May 2026, a new mandatory registration requirement for paid tax advisers interacting with HMRC on behalf of clients rolls out in stages through 31 March 2027. Different adviser groups have staggered three-month windows to register: new advisers from 18 May, those with Self Assessment or Corporation Tax accounts from 18 August, payroll-only advisers from 18 November, and existing ASA holders from 31 December 2026. Eligible advisers who do not register by their deadline, or who fail to meet required standards, will not be permitted to interact with HMRC on behalf of clients, and HMRC may apply sanctions including financial penalties.

Source: HMRC

PRA to consult on ring-fence shared services reform

The PRA announced on 18 May 2026 that it will publish a consultation this summer proposing greater flexibility for ring-fenced banks in sharing operational resources such as data-processing, IT, and back-office functions across the group. The ring-fencing regime, introduced in 2019, applies to any bank with over 35 billion pounds of core deposits that undertakes material investment banking activity. The PRA states the reform will streamline requirements and unlock cost savings for firms, supported by advances in the UK's bank resolution regime since 2019.

Source: Bank of England
