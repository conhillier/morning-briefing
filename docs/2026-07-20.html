1. US strikes Iran for ninth night running

The US Central Command completed its ninth consecutive night of strikes against Iran at 10 p.m. ET on Sunday. The three-hour operation targeted Iranian military command centers, air defense and coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, missile and drone launch sites. Iranian authorities said Sunday that at least 50 people were killed and more than 500 wounded in the renewed US strikes this month. A third American service member was killed in recent fighting, bringing the total number of US personnel killed in the nearly five-month war to 17.

Why it matters: Hormuz traffic has slowed to its lowest level in three weeks, with only eight passings on Thursday compared to 15 a day earlier, and Brent crude futures climbed almost 3% to about $90.7 a barrel.

Source: CNBC

2. Iran drones hit US bases in Kuwait

The US embassy in Bahrain's capital, Manama, said Monday it has information suggesting Iran may seek to target unspecified locations in the city, with sirens sounding across Bahrain as authorities urged residents to take shelter. Tehran has also retaliated across the Gulf with fresh attacks on targets in Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps said Monday that two oil tankers "exploded" attempting to travel through the "unsafe and dangerous route south of the Strait of Hormuz."

Why it matters: The warring powers have intensified their attacks in the region, stoking fears of a return to full war, with Washington and Tehran accusing each other of violating a memorandum of understanding reached in June.

Source: CNBC

3. Russia launches massive missile strike on Ukraine

Russia carried out a wave of ballistic missile and drone strikes on Ukraine, killing at least eight people and injuring dozens, officials say. Four people were killed in Kharkiv, an elderly woman died in Kyiv, two more fatalities occurred in Zaporizhzhia, and one in Sumy. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky described the raid on Kyiv as "one of the most massive ballistic attacks" since Russia launched a full-scale invasion in 2022. The Ukrainian military said air defences in the capital shot down 18 out of 41 missiles and intercepted 108 drones.

Source: BBC

4. Thames Water creditors seek Burnham talks

The group of investors pursuing a rescue bid for Thames Water have said they are willing to discuss greater public control but are also preparing for a potential multi-billion pound legal battle. London & Valley Water (L&VW), a consortium of 100 institutional investors holding £17bn of Thames Water's £21bn debt, said it is open to government involvement but not public ownership. The consortium has hired top litigation and disputes firm Pallas Partners to work alongside Akin Gump amid reports that Burnham could put Thames Water into a special administration regime.

Why it matters: Thames Water serves 16 million customers in London and the Thames valley and the future of the company will be one of the most pressing issues in Burnham's in-tray when he enters Downing Street.

Source: The Guardian

5. TSMC adds $100bn to Arizona expansion

TSMC is scaling up its investment in Arizona by committing an additional $100 billion to aggressively expand its US chipmaking footprint, raising its total investment pipeline in Arizona to $265 billion. The commitment has also driven an upward revision to the company's full-year capital expenditure to between $60 billion and $64 billion.

Why it matters: US fab construction costs are four to five times higher than in Taiwan, but Huang said the expansion will ultimately further foster the development of the US semiconductor ecosystem.

Source: CNBC

Closer to Home

Arthur's Seat fire breaks out in Edinburgh

Fire crews are battling a large blaze on Arthur's Seat in Edinburgh after the fire broke out below Salisbury Crags at about 18:40 on Sunday. A huge plume of white smoke was visible from as far away as Fife, with six appliances in attendance. The Scottish Fire and Rescue Service said there were "no reported casualties" and Police Scotland closed Queens Drive to all vehicles. The incident is the latest wildfire callout for the Scottish fire service, coming as hundreds of firefighters, two helicopters and specialist resources remain deployed to the Cairngorms National Park where a separate blaze has been ongoing for four days.

Source: BBC
