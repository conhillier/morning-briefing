Sunday 07 June 2026 · 4-minute read

1. Ukraine Launches Massive Drone Strike on St Petersburg

Russia declared Ukraine's attack on and around St Petersburg 'unprecedented', with more than 140 drones shot down over the surrounding Leningrad region. Ukrainian President Zelensky said his forces hit Russia's arsenals and a naval base, with drones covering 1,000km to target 'the enemy navy's arsenals and a base in Kronstadt'. Russia's defence ministry said air defence units had shot down or intercepted 339 Ukrainian drones over multiple regions since 07:00 local time on Saturday. The attacks came a day after Putin refused Zelensky's request for a face-to-face meeting.

Why it matters: Zelensky is expected to visit the UK on Sunday for talks with Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer, German Chancellor Friedrich Merz and French President Emmanuel Macron.

Source: BBC

2. US Eyes Iranian Assets for Gulf Allies' Reconstruction

US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent has directed a team to assess the costs of damages inflicted on Gulf allies by Iran, with the US also considering using Iranian assets to repair future destruction. The disclosure came a day after an adviser to Iran's supreme leader told CNN that a peace deal hinged on the release of $24 billion in Iranian assets frozen by the United States. US forces also struck Iranian coastal radar sites in Goruk and Qeshm Island, both in the Strait of Hormuz, after shooting down Iranian drones that posed a threat to maritime traffic. A Pakistani minister arrived in Tehran on Saturday carrying a 'special letter' from Pakistan's army chief and prime minister to Khamenei.

Why it matters: The threatened redirection of Iranian assets could create a new irritant to a fragile ceasefire between the United States and Iran, which was tested again this weekend with strikes by the US and Iran.

Source: CNBC

3. Xi Jinping Makes Rare Trip to North Korea

Chinese President Xi Jinping is visiting Pyongyang on Sunday, his first trip to North Korea since 2019. The visit is seen as significant given Xi's reduced travel in recent years - he averaged about 14 trips a year between 2013 and 2019 but dropped to approximately six a year between 2022 and 2025. Analysts say Xi may be travelling amid concerns about North Korea's deepening relationship with Russia, with South Korea's Institute for National Security Strategy estimating Moscow has paid North Korea as much as $14.4 billion for troop deployments and weapons exports since 2023.

Why it matters: South Korean Minister of Unification Chung Dong-young told reporters last month that he expects the two leaders to discuss a possible meeting between Kim and Trump later in the year.

Source: Al Jazeera

4. Israeli Strikes Kill Nine in Lebanon Including Army Officers

Israeli airstrikes on southern Lebanon on Saturday killed nine people, including three members of the Lebanese military. A strike on a vehicle on a road linking Nabatiyeh with Marjayoun killed a brigadier general, a captain and another soldier, while a separate strike on the village of Saksakiyah killed six people and wounded four. Lebanon's President Joseph Aoun called the strike 'a flagrant violation to Lebanese sovereignty and international law.' The attacks came days after a new US-brokered ceasefire deal, which Hezbollah has refused.

Source: NPR

5. CDC Warns DRC Ebola Could Match 2014 Record Outbreak

The US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention published modelling scenarios on Friday projecting the DRC Ebola outbreak could grow to between 10,000 and more than 20,000 cases, potentially matching the scale of the 2014-2016 west Africa outbreak that killed more than 11,000 people. The Africa CDC said there have been about 400 confirmed cases, including 63 deaths. The outbreak response has been complicated by armed conflict and attacks by the Islamic State-affiliated Allied Democratic Forces. There are no specific treatments or vaccines for the Bundibugyo virus at the heart of the current outbreak.

Why it matters: The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a global health emergency in May, and experts say there are probably other cases that have not been diagnosed or reported.

Source: The Guardian
