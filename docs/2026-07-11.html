1. US-Iran talks set for Oman Saturday

2. Zaporizhzhia under threat as Russian forces close in

Emergency crews are searching for survivors after a Russian air strike on the Ukrainian city of Zaporizhzhia killed at least one person and injured 29, including two children. The city's mayor says Russian troops have advanced to just over 20 kilometres away.

Source: Al Jazeera

3. SK Hynix surges 13% on Nasdaq debut

SK Hynix rose 13% in its first day of trading on Nasdaq, closing at $168.01, as US investors jumped at the opportunity to buy into South Korea's second-most valuable company. The company's American depositary receipts were priced at $149, raising $26.5 billion for expansion plans including new factories and equipment. The company has announced a $4 billion advanced packaging plant in Indiana, while a cluster of chip fabrication plants in Yongin, South Korea will cost $390 billion.

Why it matters: SK Hynix's valuation has risen more than sevenfold over the past year as demand for AI infrastructure has caused a shortage in computer memory and sent prices skyrocketing.

Source: CNBC

4. Apple sues OpenAI over trade secret theft

Apple has sued OpenAI in federal court in Northern California, alleging trade secret theft and claiming the AI lab took the iPhone maker's intellectual property to develop its own consumer hardware. Apple alleged that OpenAI's hardware chief, Tang Tan, a former Apple vice president, directed Apple employees interviewing at OpenAI to share Apple secrets, including bringing "actual parts" from Apple to interviews for "show and tell" sessions.

Why it matters: It is a shocking reversal for the two companies, which entered into a high-profile partnership in 2024 when ChatGPT was integrated into the iPhone's operating system, and mounting legal woes present another risk to OpenAI as it gears up for what is expected to be a historic IPO.

Source: CNBC

5. Spain wildfire kills 12 including four Britons

Emergency services in south-eastern Spain are working to contain pockets of fire after one of the country's worst ever wildfires, with 12 people killed around the village of Bedar in Almeria province, among them four Britons according to Spanish authorities. Another 23 people are still missing, and local officials in the Los Gallardos area have warned the number of dead could rise, with fears that more Britons are among those killed. The fire broke out on Thursday afternoon, driven by soaring temperatures of around 40C (104F), incredibly dry ground and powerful winds, with the cause put down to a fallen power line, though local electricity companies have denied this. The Andalusian weather agency said conditions were due to be "rather more favourable" on Saturday, with moderate wind and cooler air.

Why it matters: With at least 12 people dead, this is already among the deadliest wildfires in Spanish history, with Andalusia's health and emergencies minister saying the majority or even all of the victims may have been foreign nationals.

Source: BBC

Closer to Home

King and Queen to open Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games

King Charles and Queen Camilla are set to attend Glasgow's 2026 Commonwealth Games opening ceremony at the OVO Hydro on 23 July, officially kicking off 10 days of sporting action. Scottish singers KT Tunstall and Nathan Evans will headline the ceremony, alongside Callum Beattie and Nina Nesbitt, with the event broadcast live by 5. Sir Chris Hoy, one of Scotland's most decorated athletes, will also take part and said: "A home Games is always a special occasion and I have so many wonderful memories from competing in the Commonwealth Games myself." All 74 batons from the King's baton relay will be reunited in Glasgow for the first time during the ceremony, where the King will read aloud the message he placed into the King's Scotland baton on 10 March 2025.

Source: BBC
