1. Zelensky calls Putin to face-to-face talks

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky published an open letter to Vladimir Putin proposing a face-to-face meeting to end the war, saying it would be "wrong to simply wait" until the war in Europe returns to the centre of US attention. Zelensky called for a full ceasefire for the duration of proposed negotiations and suggested Switzerland or Turkey as possible venues. The Kremlin confirmed it had received the letter and that Putin would be briefed on it, while US President Donald Trump said "it would be great" if the two leaders met. Putin, speaking in St Petersburg, said he was "certainly prepared and willing to reach an agreement with Ukraine" but insisted compromises needed to be made.

Why it matters: Ceasefire negotiations have stalled in recent months, and previous peace talks in Geneva, Abu Dhabi and Istanbul have failed.

Source: BBC

2. Hezbollah rejects Israel-Lebanon ceasefire deal

Hezbollah leader Naim Qassem emphatically rejected the terms of a US-backed ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon, calling the negotiations "futile" and "humiliating" and saying the deal amounted to surrender. The agreement, reached after a fourth round of US-mediated talks in Washington, requires the evacuation of all Hezbollah operatives from an area between the Israeli border and the Litani river, approximately 30km to the north. It also involves the creation of pilot security zones inside Lebanon where Hezbollah operatives would be banned. The two countries' representatives are due to meet again on 22 June for further talks.

Why it matters: With Hezbollah - Lebanon's most powerful armed group - rejecting the deal outright, the prospect of a comprehensive agreement remains highly uncertain despite US brokering efforts.

Source: BBC

3. North Korea unveils new nuclear fuel plant

North Korea disclosed a new facility to produce nuclear bomb fuels, with leader Kim Jong Un announcing plans to bolster the country's nuclear forces "at an exponential rate." South Korea's Joint Chiefs of Staff assessed the site as a uranium enrichment plant and said it was closely coordinating with the United States to monitor North Korean nuclear activities. It is the third time North Korea has disclosed a uranium enrichment site, with experts assessing the newly revealed facility as likely an additional enrichment plant at the Yongbyon complex. Kim also claimed that North Korea's nuclear materials production capacity has more than doubled compared with five years ago.

Source: NPR

4. Rebel attacks hamper DRC Ebola response

Islamic State-linked militia the Allied Democratic Forces (ADF) killed more than 30 people in raids around the city of Beni in North Kivu, eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, complicating the response to the ongoing Ebola outbreak. As of Wednesday, 344 cases and 60 deaths have been recorded in the Ebola outbreak across North Kivu, South Kivu and Ituri provinces. The military governor of North Kivu confirmed that three patients with confirmed Ebola had fled treatment centres in Beni following Saturday's attacks. Community leaders warned that people fleeing the violence increased the risk of the disease spreading between communities.

Why it matters: Civilians fleeing ADF attacks make it difficult to enforce containment measures, raising the risk that the Ebola virus could spread from one community to another.

Source: The Guardian

5. Anthropic revenue hits $47bn annualised rate

Ahead of its IPO, Anthropic co-founder Daniela Amodei said the company's annualised revenue crossed $47 billion in May, up dramatically from roughly $9 billion at the end of 2025.

Why it matters: Amodei argued that businesses are still early in deploying AI effectively, and that more value will be realised as the business community grows more familiar with the tools - a case she is making to public market investors.

Source: TechCrunch

Closer to Home

Amazon brings same-day groceries to UK

Amazon is expanding its ultra-fast Amazon Now delivery service - which delivers goods in less than 30 minutes - to Manchester and Birmingham this year, and will add fresh fruit, vegetables, meat, dairy and frozen foods to same-day deliveries for shoppers in parts of central and east London. The same-day grocery service will be a free option for Amazon Prime members on orders over £20, while non-members pay a £5.99 delivery fee. Amazon, which booked UK sales of about £32bn last year, has said it will invest £40bn in the UK over three years from 2025.

Source: The Guardian
