1. Roof collapse kills 14 children in Pakistan

Fourteen children died after the roof of a building near Lahore collapsed on top of them at a private tuition centre in the Kahna suburb. Emergency service Rescue 1122 received a call about the incident at 16:45 local time, and the rescue operation was completed within one hour. Most people recovered were between seven and 11 years old, and another five people are thought to be injured. Authorities launched an investigation and two people were taken into custody as part of the initial inquiry.

Source: BBC

2. Venezuela toddler rescued six days after quake

A three-year-old boy named Klieber Moran was pulled alive from rubble in La Guaira state six days after the devastating earthquakes in Venezuela. The death toll from last week's quakes - with magnitudes of 7.2 and 7.5 - has risen to 1,943, with more than 10,000 people injured and tens of thousands more unaccounted for. The massive tremors probably damaged or destroyed 58,870 buildings, according to an initial assessment of satellite data from Nasa. The UN's refugee agency said food shortages were widespread, basic services had broken down, and communications had been largely severed in La Guaira, and said it needed an initial $15m to scale up support for 30,000 earthquake-affected people over six months.

Why it matters: The WHO warned health services were under "extreme pressure" and that there was "an increased risk now of outbreaks of vaccine-preventable diseases" such as measles and diphtheria due to low vaccination coverage.

Source: BBC

3. US lifts export ban on Anthropic AI models

Anthropic said it will begin restoring access on Wednesday after being notified that the US Department of Commerce has lifted restrictions. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick wrote in a letter that "Anthropic has agreed to proactively detect and address security risks associated with the models" and will alert the government of any malicious activity. The two models had been abruptly suspended on 12 June over concerns they could be used by hackers to exploit weaknesses in computer systems.

Why it matters: The Commerce Department reserves the right to reconsider its decision to lift export restrictions if necessary, signalling continued government oversight of advanced AI tools.

Source: BBC

4. Chinese tycoon Guo Wengui jailed 30 years for fraud

Self-exiled Chinese billionaire Guo Wengui was sentenced to 30 years in a US prison for a massive financial fraud that Judge Analisa Torres said cost over 1,000 people worldwide hundreds of millions of dollars.

Why it matters: China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs noted the sentencing and confirmed that Guo is wanted by the Chinese government and has an Interpol "Red Notice" on him.

Closer to Home

UK defence plan targets drone warships for Scotland

The UK government's Defence Investment Plan commits nearly 300bn over four years, including 5bn for a radical "drone transformation" of the armed forces featuring a "hybrid fleet" of crewed and uncrewed vessels. The plan includes at least six CCV motherships to replace the current Type 45 destroyers, with concept designs for uncrewed warships some of which are about 300ft (100m) long. Scottish shipyards in Glasgow and Rosyth have dominated construction of UK surface warships for decades, with BAE Systems yards at Govan and Scotstoun fulfilling orders for 13 Type-26 anti-submarine frigates - keeping them busy until 2038 at the earliest. Babcock's marine division chief Sir Nick Hine said the Defence Investment Plan is "positive for Babcock, it's positive for UK industry and it's positive for Rosyth."

Source: BBC

For CFOs - UK regulatory updates

Companies House accounts filing reforms delayed to April 2028

From April 2028, all UK registered companies must file accounts in iXBRL format using commercial software, with web and paper-based filing systems closing for accounts filings. Small companies and micro-entities will be required to file profit and loss accounts but may opt out of publication on the public register; abridged accounts will no longer be permitted. Companies House confirmed the implementation date has moved from April 2027 to April 2028, giving companies one full accounting year plus 9 months (21 months) to prepare.

Source: Companies House
