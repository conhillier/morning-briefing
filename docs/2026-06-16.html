1. B-52 crash kills eight at California base

Eight people, including two Boeing employees, were killed after a US Air Force B-52 bomber crashed immediately after take-off from Edwards Air Force Base in southern California. The incident occurred at 11:20 local time (19:20 GMT) on Monday while the aircraft had been on a routine test mission. Col James Hayes described the victims as a "mixed crew of military, government civilians and government contractors" and said the crash was "totally contained" within the base on the runway. No cause has yet been determined and will not be until after a series of investigations, which could take up to 30 days.

Why it matters: The base has temporarily grounded operations, and further cause analysis investigations could take more than six months.

Source: BBC

2. Iran deal framework: Vance says release imminent

US Vice-President JD Vance says President Trump may decide to release the preliminary US-Iran deal before Friday, when it is formally set to be signed in Geneva. Vance described the memorandum of understanding as "about a page and a half" and a "very general" document, adding that many details would be worked out during future technical negotiations. Senior US officials confirmed the deal was signed electronically by Trump, Vance, and Iranian Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf. The deal will extend a ceasefire for another 60 days, during which the sides will negotiate a final agreement, and the Strait of Hormuz is expected to re-open on Friday.

Why it matters: Technical talks on Iran's nuclear programme are expected to begin this week, though sanctions relief or release of assets will depend on Iran meeting its commitments under the deal.

Source: BBC

3. G7 opens with Ukraine and Middle East top of agenda

Leaders at the Group of Seven summit in Evian-les-Bains, France, face their first full day of talks on Tuesday, including discussions on ending Russia's war in Ukraine, with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy attending at the invitation of host France. Hours before the summit opened, Russia fired hundreds of drones and dozens of missiles at Ukraine's biggest cities in a barrage that killed 11 people and set fire to a religious landmark. French President Macron said he would seek to persuade Trump to continue supporting Ukraine and increase pressure on Russia to reach a peace agreement. Leaders of Egypt, Qatar and the United Arab Emirates are joining a special session focused on Middle East stability.

Source: NPR

4. SpaceX IPO raised $85.7bn after greenshoe clause

SpaceX raised $10bn more than initially thought when it sold shares to the public, bringing in a total of $85.7bn after banks exercised a so-called "greenshoe" clause allowing them to purchase an extra $10bn of shares. The underwriters, which included Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, and JPMorgan, exercised the option in full, purchasing an additional 83.3 million shares directly from the company to meet huge demand. SpaceX shares surged by more than 19% to $192 on Monday, having first been offered to investors at $135.

Why it matters: Because the vast majority of Musk's wealth is directly tied up in SpaceX equity, his trillionaire status remains entirely dependent on the market, and a sharp decline in the stock could strip him of the title just as quickly as continued gains could multiply it.

Source: BBC

5. UK bans under-16s from social media from 2027

The UK government has announced it will ban under-16s from accessing a range of social media platforms, with Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer saying he hoped "to pass regulation before Christmas," with the ban coming into force in spring 2027. Apps including TikTok, Snapchat, Instagram, YouTube, Facebook and X will become inaccessible for children, while messaging apps such as WhatsApp and Signal will not be banned. The government said over-16s may have to verify their ages to continue using social media platforms, though many adults would not need to do so as their accounts are already verified. A consultation that closed last month received more than 116,000 responses, with 90% of parents who responded backing the ban.

Source: BBC

Closer to Home

UK seizes Russian shadow fleet tanker in Channel

British Royal Marines commandos and National Crime Agency officers boarded the tanker Smyrtos in a predawn raid on Sunday, descending onto the vessel with ropes from Chinook helicopters, in the first UK-led operation to board and detain a vessel from Russia's so-called shadow fleet. The tanker was carrying 700,000 barrels of Russian oil and sailing under a Cameroonian flag, having departed the Russian Baltic port of Ust-Luga on June 5 bound for Port Said in Egypt. Following the raid, at least six other tankers immediately changed course away from the English Channel, and an Indian national was arrested on suspicion of sanctions offences. The UK Ministry of Defence said the UK has imposed sanctions on more than 500 vessels linked to Russia's shadow fleet, and the measures contributed to a 24% decline in Russian oil and gas revenues in 2025 compared with the previous year.

Source: Al Jazeera
